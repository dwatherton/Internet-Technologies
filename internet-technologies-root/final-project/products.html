Products

As a beginning skateboarder or as a seasoned verteran you will always have the need to find new skateboard parts, and we know, it can be tough. Whether you aren't sure what all you will need, or are just looking for some more information we are here to help you! For the new skateboarders please refer to the image to the right for a complete diagram of the parts discussed on this page.

As part of our promise to help, we have decided to compile a table of skateboarding related parts. Listed below you will find the parts name, what it is used for, and how much to expect to pay. Note: The typical costs listed below are only ballpark numbers, real prices may be higher or lower.

Part Name What It's Used For Typical Cost
Deck The "deck" of the skateboard is the wooden surface of the skateboard that you ride on. $30.00
Trucks The "trucks" of the skateboard are the metal 'axels' that connect the wheels to the deck. $25.00
Wheels The "wheels" of the skateboard are the polyeurothane 'tires' of the skateboard. $20.00
Bearings The "bearings" are the tiny metal cylinders that help the wheels spin with less friction. $20.00
Griptape The "Griptape" is the thin black colored 'sandpaper' layer that goes on to top of the deck. $10.00
Hardware The "hardware" is essentially just the screws and bolts that hold the entire thing together. $5.00
Risers The "risers" are little rubber rectangles that act as shock absorbers between the trucks and the deck. $3.00

All of these parts, their names, and what they cost can be a lot to take in, and no doubt confusing at first, but if you stick with it, you can become a master in no time. We hope this information has been helpful to you.

For additional information on any of the parts listed above you can check out Warehouse Skateboards Buying Guide for an even further in depth elaboration.
